How to Declutter

Before thinking about how you want to decorate a room, you first need to declutter. This is usually the most tiresome and annoying part of the process of redecorating a space. However, it is the most important step. Fortunately, we can lessen the difficulty of decluttering with this four step guide.

Source: Information on this page came from Refined Rooms

STEP 1: EVALUATE

Start your project by determining the “mission” of the space/room. Don’t skip this step. You need to determine specifically how you want this space to serve you so you’ll know what things ultimately belong in that space. Ask yourself: What functions will this space serve? Is it a multi-purpose space? Perhaps you also use your kitchen table as a homework station/craft area for your young kids. If so, then it makes sense for craft supplies to be stored in the kitchen.

STEP 2: SORT

Step 1 is all about evaluating the space you are organizing. In step 2, you continue to wear your “evaluation hat”, but now you will be evaluating each item that’s currently in the space. You will need to set up a sorting area and get a few containers (a trash bag or boxes work! Use whatever you feel works better!). Each container will be labeled "trash", "donate", and "keep", respectively. Once you are done, take every item out and begin sorting into the three categories.

STEP 3: PURGE

This step is the hardest. It’s time to make the hard decisions as to whether each item deserves to occupy precious space in your home. The questions that you should be asking yourself during the purging process are somewhat dependent upon the specific space you’re organizing. There are some general questions that can help you to make good decisions: Do I love this? How often do I use this? How many of these do I have?

STEP 4: ORGANIZE

Now, you get to assign each item a home and put systems in place for storing and retrieving your items. Separate the items that you decided to keep from "used often" to "least used." This will give you an idea of what should be handy. Then, put those items into categories that work for you (type of item, color, or amount) and use organizing tools, such as dividers and labels, to figure out the placing within the space you chose. Voila! You are done!
